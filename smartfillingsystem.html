What is Smart Filling and Pricing System?

This Smart Filling and Pricing System is a system that is capable of filling containers using a dc pump.

It measures the amount of litres filled by the dc pump and calculates the price for those litres.



Yes you have seen this filling machine on Petrol Stations where you give the number of litres you want to fill or give amount of money you want to fill with. It then does its thing and calculates what needs to be calculated depending on what the user entered. (money/litres)

    • Determining the number of litres filled per specific time

    To determine how many litres this DC pump pumps per specific time, a stop watch was used and time was recorded down.

    Findings

    After experimenting, it was found that the pump pumps 100ml per 3 seconds.
    Note This, of course, will differ with respect to the diameter of the tube and the voltage applied to the pump.

    We suggest you do your own experiment using a stopwatch and record down your own findings.

    Components functions

    8 x Push Buttons

    1. reset the device or system
    2. manually fill a container
    3. go to settings
    4. increment (go up arrow) a number
    5. decrement (go down arrow) a number
    6. navigate to the left
    7. navigate to the right
    8. Ok, to save or continue

    Liquid Crystal Display

    - display the data on the screen.

    • gives instructions to the user
    • displays the number of litres filled
    • displays the amount of money to be paid
    • displays the settings


    Arduino Uno

    - to take instructions from the user and executive them as instructed.


    SPST Relay

    - to open and close everytime it recieves a signal, it will help in introducing a voltage source that is higher than what arduino can supply.


    Diode

    to protect arduino from back emf by blocking current to flow back to arduino.


    Buzzer

    to make beep sound when the device is done filling.


    3x Light Emitting Diodes

    1. green, to indicate ready to fill
    2. blue, to indicate filling in progress
    3. red, to indicate done filling

    Transistor

    used as a switch to close and open the relay.


    Power Supply

    to step down 230V AC voltage and convert it to a 12V DC voltage to supply the arduino.


    Voltage Divider Circuit

    to step down the 12V DC voltage to 5V to power arduino.
